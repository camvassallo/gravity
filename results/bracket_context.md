## 6. Bracket Strategy

### Core Principles
1. **Maximize edge over the field**, not pick the "most likely" bracket
2. **Champion pick is the highest-leverage decision** (320 pts)
3. **Fade over-picked teams** where models disagree with public
4. **Pick upsets only where analyst consensus + data support them**

### Strategic Pillars

**1. CHAMPION: Michigan (14.4% public vs 18-26% model)**
- Biggest bracket value play available
- Universal analyst support (4/10 title picks, everyone has them in F4)
- Easiest draw of any 1-seed
- If Michigan wins, we beat 85% of brackets on champion alone

**2. FADE DUKE (25.3% public, -9.1% bracket value)**
- Single largest over-pick in the tournament
- Foster OUT, Ngongba GTD, guard play is the weakness
- Multiple analysts pick St. John's over them
- If Duke loses before F4, ~25% of competing brackets bust
- Pick St. John's to beat Duke in Sweet 16

**3. ILLINOIS AS SOUTH REGION SLEEPER (1.1% public, +3.6% value)**
- "Least discussed genuine national tournament threat"
- Best kill-shot team. Massive bracket value
- Fade Florida (analyst consensus bearish, tough draw)

**4. PICK CONSENSUS UPSETS THE PUBLIC ISN'T PRICING**
- VCU over UNC (near-universal analyst consensus)
- Santa Clara over Kentucky (near-universal)
- Hofstra over Alabama (strong consensus + Holloway out)
- South Florida over Louisville (strong consensus + Brown out)
- Iowa over Clemson, Utah State over Villanova

### Proposed Final Four
| Region | Pick | Public Champion % | Edge |
|---|---|---|---|
| East | St. John's | 1.1% | Duke fade busts 25% of field |
| West | Arizona | 19.6% | Most solid 1-seed, analyst favorite |
| South | Illinois | 1.3% | +3.6% bracket value, huge leverage |

**Championship: Michigan over Arizona**

### Risk Assessment
- This is an aggressive, high-leverage bracket
- If Duke wins it all, we lose badly — but 79% of brackets also don't have Duke, so the downside is bounded
- If Michigan wins, we beat ~85% of brackets on champion alone
- St. John's + Illinois in F4 differentiates from ~98% of brackets
- The upsets we're picking are the SAME ones that 6-8 out of 9 analyst sources independently identified
